# Target Users & Success Metrics — Notari

## Target users

**Primary persona — The Active Student**
- High school, college, or grad school student studying academic content (languages, sciences, humanities, professional certifications).
- Owns an iPhone and at least one other Apple device (iPad or Mac).
- Currently uses Quizlet, Anki, or paper flashcards — and is mildly dissatisfied with the experience.
- Values: clean design, no ads, real ownership of their notes, ability to study anywhere (offline included).
- Comfortable paying $5 for an app they expect to use weekly.

**Secondary persona — The Lifelong Learner**
- Adult learning a new domain (language, professional skill, hobby).
- Less price-sensitive than students; more likely to convert to the Online tier for buddy features.
- May not need social features but values the AI chatbot for explanations.

## Success metrics

### Launch (first 90 days post-App Store release)

| Metric | Target | Why |
|--------|--------|-----|
| App Store rating | ≥ 4.5 stars | Required for any organic discovery. Below 4.3 and reviews start hurting installs. |
| Day-1 → Day-7 retention | ≥ 30% | Standard benchmark for a paid productivity app. |
| Avg. decks created per user (Day 7) | ≥ 2 | Signal that users found enough value to invest in building content. |
| AI tokens consumed per active user (Day 30) | ≥ 1 paid pack | Validates the token economy works. |
